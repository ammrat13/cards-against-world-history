Instructions
Cards Against World History is a game that requires three or more players. To start a game, have one player create a room by clicking on the "Create" dropdown. Then, have that player share the PIN displayed with everyone who wants to be in the game. All the other players then click on the "Join" dropdown and enter the pin they recieved. Everyone then enters their name and clicks on "Go".

Cards Against World History was inspired by, and is thus very similar to, Cards Against Humanity. In every round, one person becomes the Card Czar and one card is dealt and displayed on the "Dealt" tab. Every player except the Card Czar then selects a card from the "Hand" tab. After all other players have selected a card, the Card Czar then selects a card from the "Field" tab, and the player who selected the card the Card Czar selected gets one point.

The primary objective of this game is to have fun. Therefore, the criteria by which the Card Czar selects cards are left unspecified and is left to the players to decide for themselves. There is also no win condition; play continues indefinitely or until everyone gets bored.
